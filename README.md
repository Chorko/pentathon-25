# Pentathon 2025

**Pentathon 2025** is a premier cybersecurity hackathon and technical bootcamp jointly organized by the **All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE)**, the **National Critical Information Infrastructure Protection Centre (NCIIPC)**, and the **Ministry of Education (MoE)**, Government of India.

## Overview

The objective of Pentathon '25 is to identify and nurture top cybersecurity talent across the nation to secure critical infrastructure and digital assets. It aims to provide a rigorous, hands-on platform where participants can test and elevate their penetration testing, vulnerability assessment, and threat mitigation skills.

### Key Organizers
- **AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education):** Driving the technical education mandate and ensuring broad participation across engineering and technical institutions.
- **NCIIPC (National Critical Information Infrastructure Protection Centre):** Providing real-world problem statements, threat intelligence scenarios, and focusing on the protection of India's Critical Information Infrastructure (CII).
- **MoE (Ministry of Education):** Supporting the initiative as part of the national capacity-building framework for digital security and innovation.

## Structure and Tracks

The bootcamp and subsequent competition are structured to simulate real-world cyber attack and defense scenarios. Participants will engage in:
- **Web Application Security:** Identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities in modern web architectures.
- **Network Security & Infrastructure:** Securing critical network infrastructure and understanding advanced persistent threats (APTs).
- **Cryptography & Reverse Engineering:** Solving complex cryptographic challenges and analyzing malicious binaries.
- **Incident Response & Forensics:** Detecting, responding to, and mitigating ongoing cyber incidents.

## Repository Contents

This repository serves as the central hub for the Pentathon '25 bootcamp materials, tracking progress across multiple days:
- `day1/`: Introduction, fundamentals of penetration testing, and basic enumeration.
- `day2/`: Advanced exploitation techniques, privilege escalation, and network pivoting.
- `day3/`: Post-exploitation, reporting, and defense-in-depth strategies.
